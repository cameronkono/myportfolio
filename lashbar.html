CLIENT:
Lashbar

ROLES:
Logomark, Identity
Lashbar is a group of salons around Southern California specializing in lash extensions and other lash services. With an empahsis on bespoke services, Lashbar technicians ensure that every job is custom and unique to their client's needs and preferences. While working with Lashbar stakeholders, I devised revisions to the brand, such as revamping the logomark, typography and more.

At Lashbar, technicians are also called 'Lash Faries' because they make magic happen. While the old logomark symbolized this concept, it was complicated and lost legibility and clarity at small sizes. Though the wordmark remains unchanged (the stakeholders' descision), the new logomark has been simplified and refined to work across a variety of contexts, while still paying homage to the Lash Fairies who make everything possible.

Just like it's service, Lashbar's typograhpy aims to be warm, yet professional. Freight Big (2005), an elegantly drawn serif by Joshua Darden, is used for larger eye-catching headings, while Sofia Pro (Olivier Gourvat, 2012) is used for small headings and body copy.
